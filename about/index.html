Academic Profile

Dr Ho Chun Loong is a Malaysian-born Assistant Professor under the Department of Biomedical Engineering at the Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech). His research focuses primarily on using synthetic biology and protein engineering to address various health and environmental issues. This includes establishing a platform where researchers could share their constructs and systematically amass these genetic constructs for protein engineering purposes. In 2013, he received his Ph.D. in biochemistry from Nanyang Technological University of Singapore, under the guidance of Professor Liang Zhao-xun. He was engaged in the production of secondary metabolites by multimodular polyketide synthase and the design of proteins as biosensors to track the intrinsic levels of cyclic di-GMP, a signal molecule in bacteria. Subsequently, he worked as a postdoctoral researcher at the Center for Clinical and Technological Innovation in Synthetic Biology (SynCTI) of the National University of Singapore, focusing on the design of proteins that endow probiotics and symbiotic microorganisms with therapeutic functions. The research results have been published in the Nature Biomedical Engineering, Journal of American Chemistry Society, and ACS Synthetic Biology, etc. More than 150 references have been made and two patents have been applied for.

Project Proposal—DNA data storage

The DNA data storage project is because the DNA fragments stored in the bacterial library usually have the problems of base loss and inconvenience of renaturation. Therefore, we have constructed a new BAC plasmid with a variety of inducible promoter factors, which can be transcribed quickly and read DNA data by sequencing. Take out the stored DNA information to achieve the purpose of information storage. The expression vector constructed in this way is not only mainly stored in Bacillus, but the sporulation characteristics of Bacillus also extend the preservation time of DNA information, and the spores can cope with various harsh environments. The corresponding mRNA can be quickly transcribed through the recovery of spores, and the inducer factor of the corresponding promoter can be added. The function of extracting information can be achieved by extracting mRNA and sequencing. Generally speaking, spores can be stored in an appropriate environment for up to 100 years, not only for long-term preservation of information, but also for the maintenance and confidentiality of important information, and various information translation can be realized on the basis of different ATGC. It has high confidentiality and is difficult to decrypt by the outside world. So, it can also be used to store military secrets or commercial information.

DNA数据存储项目是因为存储在细菌库中的DNA片段通常存在碱基丢失和复性不便的问题。因此，我们构建了具有多种诱导型启动子因子的新BAC质粒，该质粒可快速转录并通过测序读取DNA数据。取出存储的DNA信息，达到信息存储的目的。以此方式构建的表达载体不仅主要存储在芽孢杆菌中，而且芽孢杆菌的孢子形成特性也延长了DNA信息的保存时间，并且孢子可以应付各种恶劣的环境。可以通过孢子的复苏快速转录相应的mRNA，并可以添加相应启动子的诱导因子。提取信息的功能可以通过提取mRNA和测序来实现。一般而言，孢子可以在适当的环境中保存长达100年，不仅可以长期保存信息，还可以保存重要信息并保持机密性，并且可以根据ATGC不同的排序实现各种信息翻译。它具有很高的机密性，并且很难被外界解密。因此，它也可以用于存储军事机密或商业信息。

Research direction: Engineering probiotics to prevent skin-related infection
Yeast chassis development, engineering antimicrobial peptide, specific targeting

Staphylococcus aureus infections are becoming a major threat due to their wide range of antibiotic resistance. The most common skin-based microbial infection is due to Staphylococcus aureus and group A β-hemolytic streptococci that can result in various medical complications such as impetigo, folliculitis, boils. Due to its infective nature, the microbial infection is easily transmissible between individuals and from public settings, such as hospitals, schools, and office areas. Diabetic, immune-compromised patients, and HIV patients are found to be highly susceptible to Staphylococcus infections where open wounds in particularly sensitive to these infections. If not treated immediately and given proper care, these infections can progress to systemic infections where the amputation of the infected limbs is the widely-accepted approach of stemming the infection from progressing any further. Studies indicate that certain types of probiotic yeast from the Saccharomyces boulardii species can thrive on the skin and facilitate wound healing processes. I am committed to engineering probiotics Saccharomyces boulardii, expressing antimicrobial peptides and targeting pathogens specifically. Prevent skin infection and promote wound healing.

酵母底盘开发，工程抗菌肽，特异性靶击
与皮肤感染相关的死亡率从2008年到2016年几乎增加了一个数量级。最常见的皮肤微生物感染是由金黄色葡萄球菌和A组β-溶血性链球菌引起的，它们可导致各种医疗并发症，如脓疱、毛囊炎、疖子。由于其传染性，微生物感染很容易在个人之间传播，也很容易从公共场所（如医院、学校和办公区）传播。糖尿病、免疫缺陷患者和HIV患者对葡萄球菌感染高度敏感，而开放性伤口对这些感染尤其敏感。如果不立即治疗并给予适当的护理，这些感染可能会发展为全身感染，在这种情况下，切断受感染的肢体是广泛接受的阻止感染进一步发展的方法。研究表明，布拉氏酵母菌中的某些类型的益生菌酵母可以促进伤口愈。此外，这些微生物可以减轻组织炎症，减轻疼痛，抑制病变组织中的氧化应激。本人致力于工程益生酵母菌，表达抗菌杀灭肽，特异性靶击病原体。预防皮肤感染和促进伤口愈合。

Research: Detection and suppression of liver cancer using engineering commensal microbial intervention
Content: Liver cancer is one of the most common forms of malignant tumors. Early liver cancer is asymptomatic, but the presence of symptoms such as jaundice, injury/bleeding, nausea, and abdominal swelling often indicates the presence of advanced cancer. Delays in the treatment of advanced liver cancer resulted in a mortality rate of more than 40%. Therefore, the early detection of liver cancer can provide more choices for patients and improve their survival rate. However, there is a bottleneck in the early detection and prevention of liver cancer——the lack of convenient and efficient early detection tools. The project uses a known E.coli that can infiltrate into liver tissues through hepatic portal vein——Nissle 1917, as an engineered microbial carrier, making it localize on the surface of liver cancer cells, sensing the microenvironment of liver cancer cells through the method of gene regulation, inducing the expression of anticancer protein genes and thereby inhibiting the growth of tumors. At the same time, compounds in the serum are converted into visible biological dyes that change the colour of the urine to assess a patient’s risk of disease.

题目：利用工程化共生菌检测和抑制肝癌
内容：肝癌是最常见的恶性肿瘤之一，早期肝癌无明显症状，而一旦出现诸如黄疸、於伤/出血、恶心和腹部肿胀等症状往往意味着晚期癌症的发生。晚期肝癌治疗的延迟使患者的死亡率超过了40%。因此，肝癌的早期发现可为患者提供更多的选择，提高患者的生存率。但是当前肝癌的早期检测和预防面临的一个瓶颈——缺乏便捷、高效的早期检测工具。本课题利用一种已知的并能通过肝门静脉渗透进入肝组织的大肠杆菌——Nissle 1917作为工程化微生物载体，使其在肝癌细胞表面定位，通过基因调控的方法来感知肝癌细胞的微环境，诱导抗癌蛋白基因的表达从而抑制肿瘤的生长。与此同时，将血清中的化合物转化成肉眼可见的生物染料，使尿液颜色改变，以此来评估病人患病的风险。
